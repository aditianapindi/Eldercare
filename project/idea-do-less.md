# Idea: "Do Less" — Content Noise Killer

## Thesis

The product that helps people do LESS will win in an era where every other product helps them do MORE.

---

## The Problem

Every day, the internet tells you 50 things you "should" do. You do none of them — because 50 feels like zero.

Anyone trying to grow — career switchers, new managers, people learning AI, anyone upskilling — is drowning in prescriptive content: "learn these 10 skills," "do this to become X," "here's the 2026 roadmap for Y." Every article adds another "you should." None of them say "you can stop here."

**What people do today and why it fails:**

- Save articles to read later — they never read them, pile just grows
- Use ChatGPT to summarize — a shorter version of bad advice is still bad advice
- Follow "experts" on LinkedIn — now 10 experts saying 10 different things
- Buy a course — halfway through, see another "better" one, start that instead
- Ask friends — friends are also confused and share more articles

Every solution adds MORE to the pile. Nothing subtracts.

---

## What Is "Content Noise"?

Noise isn't about the topic. It's about the behavior and the outcome.

**Signal** = Content that gives you a specific action you can take this week, based on where you actually are right now.

**Noise** = Content that creates the FEELING of productivity without changing what you actually do.

| Content | Signal or Noise? | Why? |
|---------|------------------|------|
| "10 AI tools every PM needs" | Noise | You read it, feel anxious, don't install any of them |
| "How to set up Cursor in 5 minutes (step-by-step)" | Signal | You read it, you set up Cursor. Done. |
| "Why you should learn SQL in 2026" | Noise | Tells you WHAT but not HOW or WHY NOW for your situation |
| A newsletter you haven't opened in 4 weeks | Noise | Doesn't matter how good it is — you're not reading it |
| A saved article you'll "get to later" | Noise | If you didn't read it in 7 days, you won't |

The pattern: noise is content that makes you feel like you should do something but doesn't help you do it. Adds anxiety, not clarity.

## The Product

### Screen 1 — What are you trying to do?

A single question. One text box.

> "I'm trying to break into product management"
> "I want to learn AI but don't know where to start"
> "I'm a new manager and feel like I'm faking it"

Whatever they type. Natural language. No dropdowns, no categories.

### Screen 2 — Show me the noise

> "Paste the links, tips, advice, or content you've been collecting. Articles, YouTube videos, tweets, course pages — anything. The messier the better."

They dump their pile. Could be 3 links. Could be 15. Could be copy-pasted text from a newsletter.

### Screen 3 — The output (this is the product)

The AI reads everything they pasted, knows their goal, and comes back with THREE things:

**1. Your ONE move this week**

Not from any single article. From the synthesis of ALL of them, filtered against their goal and stage. One clear, specific action.

> "Write one product teardown and post it on LinkedIn. Here's why: 6 of the 8 articles you pasted agree that a portfolio matters more than certifications at your stage. This is the fastest way to start."

**2. What to keep (1-2 items max)**

The content that's actually worth their time, with a specific reason.

> "Keep this one article [link] — it has a step-by-step teardown template you can use right now."

**3. What to let go (everything else)**

The rest, with honest reasons. Not mean. Just clear.

> - "10 PM skills for 2026" — generic, nothing you can act on today
> - "SQL for PMs course" — real skill, wrong time. Revisit after you land the role
> - "How I got into Google PM" — survivor bias story, entertaining but not a roadmap for you
> - That YouTube playlist — 12 hours of content, 20 minutes of unique insight. Not now.

**The feeling the user walks away with:** Relief. Permission to ignore 80% of what they've been hoarding. And ONE clear thing to do instead of 50 vague things to feel bad about.

---

## Why This Isn't "Just Use ChatGPT"

1. **The structure is the value.** ChatGPT gives a wall of text. This gives three clear sections: one move, what to keep, what to let go. The format is designed to make you feel LIGHTER, not more informed.
2. **It's opinionated.** Doesn't say "it depends." Says "drop this, keep that, do this." People are starving for that.
3. **It's repeatable.** New pile next week? Dump it in. Get your one move. Weekly ritual.
4. **The output is shareable.** "I pasted 10 articles about learning AI into this tool. It told me to ignore 8 of them and do ONE thing this week."

---

## Why Google/Apple Can't Do This

Google, Apple, and Microsoft can triage YOUR stuff — emails, calendar, tasks inside their ecosystem. They CANNOT triage external content against YOUR personal context. Google Search ranks by popularity, not relevance-to-you. Gemini can summarize each article, but won't tell you which to SKIP or why.

## Distribution (Getting to 100 Users)

**LinkedIn DM template:**
"I've been hoarding articles about [topic] for weeks. Pasted them all into this tool. It told me to ignore 8 out of 10 and do ONE thing instead. Honestly, the relief was worth it. Try it: [link]"

**LinkedIn post template:**
"I pasted 10 articles about [topic] into this tool. It told me 8 were noise and gave me ONE action for this week. The 'what to let go' section hit hard. Try it: [link]"

**Why it spreads:**
- Everyone has a pile of unread articles and the same guilt
- The output is contrarian (tells you to consume LESS) — sparks engagement
- "Do less" is a counterintuitive message in a world of "do more"
- Applicable to anyone trying to grow — not gated to one niche
