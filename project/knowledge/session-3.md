# Session 3 — 2026-04-07

## What was built
- **Financial pain validation document** — docs/financial-pain-validation.md
  - 27+ direct quotes from real people across AgingCare, AARP, Slate, ETV Bharat, Grapevine.in, Medium, Trustpilot
  - 7 workarounds mapped with failure modes (WhatsApp, Google Sheets, Splitwise, mental accounting, bank access, BBPS, CA)
  - 30+ hard statistics with sources (unclaimed assets, claim rejections, lapse rates, property disputes)
  - 3 sub-angles scored head-to-head with clear recommendation
  - 6 "hair on fire" trigger moments documented with evidence
  - Dead/alive startup audit (Elcare dead, Yellow/WillJini/AasaanWill/Kustodian/Khyaal alive but niche)
  - Closest existing products mapped (FOLO, Zedger, WiseCare, Kustodian, SBNRI)

## Decisions made
- **Sibling expense coordination is the sharpest wedge** — validated over financial visibility and document vault
  - Lowest activation energy (no parent cooperation needed)
  - Recurring pain (monthly, not crisis-only)
  - Zero competition in India
  - Naturally expands to visibility + vault later
- **The pain is REAL, not just a logical gap** — Rs 1.84 lakh crore unclaimed, 53% of LIC policies lapse, 66% of civil cases are property disputes, 27% of caregivers depleted savings

## What broke / surprised us
- **FOLO exists** — consolidates networth across 70+ financial sources with 1-click family share. Closest to financial visibility angle. But no expense tracking or sibling coordination.
- **Zedger's "Family Book" mode** — Indian Splitwise alternative that acknowledged Splitwise doesn't work for families. Still generic, not parent-care-specific.
- **Kustodian.life has real traction** — 8,300+ families, Rs 150+ Cr recovered, 97% success rate, 90%+ word-of-mouth. Proves the post-death pain. But nobody prevents the problem.
- **Reddit/Quora block web crawlers** — couldn't get direct quotes from Indian Reddit. Manual visit needed for r/india, r/personalfinanceindia threads.
- **The chronic vs acute tension** — chronic pain (monthly tracking) is 7/10. Acute pain (hospital, death) is 10/10. Product must serve both.
