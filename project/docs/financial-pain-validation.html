Current Solutions

What Families Use Today — and Why Each Fails

Every workaround solves a fragment. None provide the full picture.

WhatsApp Groups

Bills shared as photos, UPI screenshots, voice notes. The default coordination layer.

Why it fails: Important info buried under "Good Morning" images. No search, no totals, no accountability. "Prescriptions get buried in hundreds of messages." — WiseCare

Gap: Communication without structure

Google Sheets

Organized sibling creates shared sheet with Date / Amount / Category / Paid By.

Why it fails: Adoption dies in 2-3 months. "The best family budget Google Sheets template is useless if only one person updates it." Not mobile-friendly for parents or helpers.

Gap: Structure without adoption

Splitwise

Siblings log expenses hoping to track who owes whom.

Why it fails: Assumes equal peers splitting equally. Shows Mom "owes" Dad. No concept of "contribution capacity." Parents are beneficiaries, not group members. Creates awkward debt dynamics in families.

Gap: Wrong model entirely (peers, not family fund)

Mental Accounting (i.e., Nothing)

"I just send money when they ask." No system, no tracking, no coordination.

Why it fails: Parents don't ask (pride). Invisible resentment builds. Nobody knows the real monthly cost. Crisis reveals total information void.

Gap: Nothing at all

Bank Account Access

Joint accounts, PoA, checking parents' bank statements.

Why it fails: PSU banks require branch visits. Scattered across 3-5 banks. See transactions but not context ("Rs 8,000 ATM withdrawal" — for what?). Sibling trust issues.

Gap: Transactions without context

Bill Payment (NRIWAY, BBPS)

NRIs pay utility bills cross-border.

Why it fails: Bills are <20% of parent expenses. Pays bills but no visibility into total spending. No coordination among siblings. Biggest expenses (medical, helpers) flow through parents' accounts.

Gap: Payment execution without awareness

CA / Accountant

Hired for annual tax filing, sometimes investment advice.

Why it fails: Engaged too late (usually after death). Annual, not ongoing. No family coordination role. Doesn't track monthly expenses or sibling contributions.

Gap: Compliance without management

The Core Gap

No product offers a single shared view where siblings can see all parent expenses, contribute proportionally based on capacity (not equally), and track who paid what — without requiring the elderly parent to use an app.

Winner: Sibling Expense Coordination

Lowest activation energy (no parent cooperation needed). Recurring engagement (monthly touchpoints). Zero competition in India. Emotionally viral on LinkedIn. Naturally expands into visibility + vault later.

1 Why NOT Financial Visibility?

Requires the parent to sit down and list every account, FD, LIC policy, PPF, pension detail. Indian parents culturally resist this conversation. You'd be asking users to have the hardest family conversation BEFORE they get any value. Kustodian already operates here with 8,300+ families and 90%+ word-of-mouth.

2 Why NOT Document Vault?

DigiLocker exists (government-backed, free, 350M+ users). A vault is fundamentally "homework" — nobody wakes up excited to digitize LIC policies. Only becomes urgent AFTER a crisis, when it's too late. The buildathon needs users NOW, not users who will need the product someday.

Trigger Moments

When It Goes From Annoying to Emergency

Critical

Hospital Admission

  • Scramble for insurance papers nobody can locate
  • Rush to nearest hospital — discover it's out-of-network
  • Emergency admission needs TPA ID + ₹10,000 deposit within 24 hours
  • NRI children get a missed call during a crisis — distance becomes painful
  • Documentation errors cause insurer rejection
"Every year, hundreds of parents of NRIs die from sudden heart attacks with only friends at their bedside."Little India
Critical

Parent's Death — The Financial Tsunami

  • Nobody knows where all the accounts are — no central depository in India
  • Nominee vs legal heir confusion (Supreme Court: nominee = trustee, NOT owner)
  • Bank accounts freeze immediately upon death notification
  • Succession Certificate: 4-6 months minimum, 12+ if disputed
  • ₹3.5+ lakh crore sits unclaimed because families never organized
High

Insurance Renewal Missed

  • Health insurance grace period: only 7-15 days, then policy lapses completely
  • All accumulated bonuses lost. Waiting periods restart. Parent may become uninsurable.
  • LIC: if term plan lapses before 3 years, every premium paid is forfeited
  • Root cause: Nobody tracked the renewal date
High

Mental Decline / Dementia

  • ~80% of people with dementia cannot manage their own money
  • Finance issues are often one of the FIRST signs (bills piling up, freezing at ATMs)
  • Nobody knows passwords, PINs, or account details
  • PoA must be established BEFORE mental incapacity — in India, PoA becomes void upon incapacity
Medium

Tax Filing Season

  • NRIs face 20-30% TDS deductions that often exceed actual liability
  • After parent's death, legal heir must register as "representative assessee" — obscure process
  • PAN-Aadhaar linkage failures block filing entirely
Medium

Property Decisions

  • Selling requires unanimous sibling consent — one holdout = legal dispute
  • No will = distribution governed by religion-specific succession laws
  • 66% of civil cases = property disputes. Avg resolution: 20 years.

Synthesis

The Strategic Play

Lead with sibling expense coordination (chronic, recurring, no parent cooperation)

Promise readiness for the acute moment ("When the hospital call comes, you'll have everything in one place"). Expand to visibility and vault once the coordination habit is established.

The key risk to manage

Chronic pain (monthly expense tracking) is a 7/10 — tolerable. Acute pain (hospital, death) is a 10/10 — but rare. The product must serve both: build for the chronic, be ready for the acute.

Why Sibling Coordination Wins

1 Lowest activation energy

Does NOT require parent cooperation. One sibling creates a shared tracker, invites others, starts logging. No uncomfortable conversations needed to start.

2 Recurring engagement

Every medical bill, utility bill, caregiver salary, and medicine purchase creates a touchpoint. Critical for an MVP needing 100 users in 10 days.

3 Zero competition

No product exists for siblings coordinating parent care expenses in India. Splitwise doesn't fit. Zedger is generic. The NRI-local sibling dynamic is culturally specific and unsolved.

4 Emotionally viral

"My brother in the US thinks sending money is enough" and "My sister never visits but has opinions" are LinkedIn-ready narratives. Maps directly to buildathon distribution needs.

5 Natural expansion

Once siblings track expenses together, the next question is "But what does Mom actually have?" (visibility) and "Where is the insurance policy?" (vault). Coordination creates demand for the other two.
