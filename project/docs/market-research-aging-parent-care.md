# Market Research: Aging Parent Care in India (Adult Child as Buyer)

*Compiled April 2026. All figures sourced from public data, company websites, investor reports, and government surveys.*

## 1. TAM / SAM / SOM Analysis

### India's Elderly Population (60+)
- **Current (2025-26):** ~153-160 million (source: PIB India, UNFPA)
- **2031 projection:** 193.4 million (Population Projections Report 2020)
- **2036 projection:** ~230 million (15% of total population)
- **2050 projection:** 347 million (UNFPA)
- Growth rate: 40% from 2021-2031 vs. 8% general population growth

### Adult Children Managing Aging Parents (Sandwich Generation)
- **36% of Indian older adults have at least one migrant child** (LASI Wave 1, 72,000+ respondents)
- **35% of elderly are "empty nesters"** — children have moved away (LASI data)
- **27% of elderly live alone or with spouse only** (up from 22% in 2011) (LASI 2017-18)
- **5.1% of elderly live completely alone** (LASI)
- **~15 million elderly live entirely alone**, three-fourths are women (Census data)
- Implied sandwich generation: At India's current 153M elderly x average ~2 adult children = **~200-250 million adult children with at least one aging parent**
- Active caregiving subset (those with parents needing regular support): **estimated 50-80 million adult children**

### NRI Population Managing Parents Remotely
- **Total Indian diaspora: 35.42 million** (15.85M NRIs + 19.57M PIOs) (MEA, May 2024)
- Top corridors: USA (5.5M), UAE (3.6M), Canada (2.87M), UK (1.86M), Saudi Arabia (2.46M)
- **Estimated NRIs with elderly parents in India: 8-12 million** (based on ~60-70% of NRIs having at least one living parent in India, extrapolated from demographic age distribution)
- Nila Cares claims immigrants spend **8+ hours/week and $5,000+/year** coordinating senior care remotely

## 3. Willingness to Pay Signals

### Caregiver Burden Data
- **35% of elderly are empty nesters** — their children have migrated
- **27% of caregivers have depleted personal savings** for eldercare costs
- NRIs spend **8+ hours/week and $5,000+/year** coordinating care remotely (Nila Cares data)
- Caregivers willing to pay for tech solutions but **price-sensitive** — prefer inexpensive apps
- App-based care platforms seeing growth post-pandemic

### "Guilt Premium" Evidence
- NRI guilt is a well-documented driver of spending on parent care services
- Every major NRI-focused eldercare provider (Nila, Anvayaa, Samarth) explicitly markets to "peace of mind" and guilt reduction
- Nila Cares: **zero customer churn** — once NRIs subscribe, they don't cancel (guilt retention)
- NRI care packages are priced at a **premium over domestic equivalents** (Nila at $100/mo vs domestic services at Rs 3,000-10,000/mo for comparable tiers)
- Concierge services TAM of $700M is described as "catering primarily to children of seniors who live in other cities or abroad, driven by guilt and anxiety"

### US/UK Benchmarks (What Adult Children Pay There)
- **US average:** Adult children spend ~26% of income on caregiving (~$7,242/year)
- **US in-home care:** $4,000+/month national average
- **US long-distance caregivers:** $8,728/year average
- **54% of US caregivers sacrifice personal spending** for parent care
- **34% of US caregivers spend $300+/month** out of pocket
- **UK assisted living:** From GBP 1,500+/month
- **Key insight:** Indian eldercare services at Rs 3,000-16,000/month are 5-20x cheaper than US/UK equivalents, creating massive willingness-to-pay headroom for NRIs

### Adjacent SaaS/Subscription Pricing
- Family finance apps (Indian market): Rs 99-499/month
- Health tracking apps: Mostly freemium with Rs 200-500/month premium
- Emoha basic tier: Rs 2,999/month (starting to normalize subscription eldercare)

## 4. Revenue Models That Work

### Startup Financial Performance

**Emoha (most data available):**
- FY25 Revenue: Rs 74.35 crore (40% YoY growth from Rs 53.21 Cr in FY24)
- FY25 Net Loss: Rs 36.68 crore (32% reduction from Rs 54.16 Cr loss in FY24)
- EBITDA margin: -48.86%
- Unit economics: **Spends Rs 1.50 to earn Rs 1 of revenue** (improving)
- Total funding raised: ~$16M (Series B: $11M led by Rainmatter Capital, Gruhas)
- Subscriber base: 60,000+ members
- Implied ARPU: ~Rs 12,400/year or ~Rs 1,033/month (revenue / subscribers)
- Employee costs: 42% of total expenses (Rs 46.8 Cr)
- **NOT yet profitable** but trajectory improving

**Anvayaa:**
- FY19 Revenue: Rs 2.5 crore
- Subscribed members: 800+
- Funding: Rs 1.5 crore (friends & family)
- Total funding: $477K
- Targeting 200% YoY growth
- Primary customers: IT professionals (NRIs)

**Nila Cares:**
- Pre-seed: $2.4M (led by LocalGlobe, Redbus Ventures)
- Monthly pricing: ~$100/month (NRI-focused)
- 3,000+ care requests in 10+ Indian cities
- **Zero customer churn** reported
- Emerged from Founders Factory x Aviva fintech studio

### What's Working: Revenue Model Comparison

| Model | Evidence | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| **Annual subscription** | Emoha (60K members), Samarth, Anvayaa all use annual billing | Primary model in market |
| **NRI premium pricing** | Nila at $100/mo, Anvayaa charges in USD | Higher ARPU from diaspora |
| **Add-on services** | Emoha condition management (Rs 1,599-14,299/mo) | Key margin driver |
| **B2B2C (employer)** | EldersIndia (India-first), Wellthy (US-first expanding to India) | Emerging, strong potential |
| **Freemium** | Most apps offer free trials, convert to paid | Works for acquisition |
| **Per-use/transactional** | Limited evidence of success as primary model | Secondary only |

### The "Who Pays" Problem — Evidence
- **The child is overwhelmingly the buyer**, not the parent
- Anvayaa: "primary demographic is IT professionals" (adult children)
- Nila: Explicitly built for "NRI children with elderly parents in India"
- Samarth: NRI contact number listed alongside India number
- Emoha: Markets to "concerned families" — the child initiates
- Concierge TAM of $700M attributed to "children of seniors living in other cities or abroad"
- ElderAid.in uses "proxy child" terminology for care managers
- **Every single provider's landing page addresses the adult child, not the senior**

## 6. The Employer Angle

### Corporate Elder Care Benefits — Current State

**India-specific:**
- **EldersIndia** — India's first B2B2C eldercare platform for corporates
  - Corporations subscribe to give employees access to eldercare services
  - Pricing: equal to or lower than individual provider rates (employer subsidy)
  - Services: home healthcare, safety, caregiving, social activities, financial security
  - Also offers employer programs: sensitization workshops, ERGs, webinars

**Global platforms expanding to India:**
- **Wellthy** (US) — launched India-specific care concierge service
  - Clients: Best Buy, Meta, Vanguard Group
  - Care coordinators fluent in English and Hindi
  - Operates across 192 countries, 132 languages
- **Torchlight** (US) — caregiver support employee benefits platform
  - Client example: Amgen (10,500 eligible employees)
  - Covers child + elderly care navigation

**Market signals:**
- Elder care is an **emerging but not yet standard** employee benefit in India
- Large employers beginning to offer: eldercare leave, counseling, care coordination platforms
- Usually bundled with flexible working arrangements
- Priced as employer subscription: per-employee annual fee
- Part of broader EAP (Employee Assistance Program) trend

### Why This Matters for a Startup
- B2B2C dramatically reduces CAC (employer pays, employee gets access)
- Enterprise contracts = predictable recurring revenue
- Employers are motivated: caregiver employees have **higher absenteeism and lower productivity**
- The employer doesn't need to be convinced of the problem — they already lose money to it

## 7. Key Takeaways for Pitch

### The Numbers That Matter

1. **Market:** $7B today, $50B by 2030. 153M elderly, growing 4x faster than general population.
2. **Buyer:** The adult child, not the parent. Every successful provider confirms this.
3. **NRI premium:** $100/month willingness-to-pay from NRIs vs. Rs 3,000-10,000 domestic. Zero churn.
4. **Remittance tailwind:** $135B inflows, >50% for family maintenance. Parent care is embedded in existing financial flows.
5. **Unit economics challenge:** Emoha at Rs 1.50 cost per Rs 1 revenue. The sector is pre-profitability but improving.
6. **Employer channel:** Nascent in India but proven in US (Wellthy, Torchlight). EldersIndia is the early mover.
7. **Investor appetite growing:** Eldercare startup funding jumped from $1.5M (2021) to $23.8M (2023). Nila raised $2.4M pre-seed for NRI focus alone.

### The Competitive Gap
- No one owns the **digital-first, lightweight coordination layer** for adult children
- Current providers are service-heavy (care managers, nursing, visits) = high marginal cost
- The tech-enabled, lower-cost-per-user model hasn't won yet
- Software layer on top of existing informal care networks is underexplored

### Pricing Sweet Spot
- **Domestic adult children:** Rs 500-3,000/month (underserved — below current market floor)
- **NRIs:** $50-150/month (proven willingness, anchored against US care costs of $4,000+/month)
- **Employers:** Rs 200-500/employee/month (as part of benefits package)
