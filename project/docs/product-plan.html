03 — Layer 1: Entry

"The Family Care Check"

An empathy-first assessment. Not "you're failing" — more "let's see where your family stands."

Tone

Warm, validating, eye-opening. Like a friend who gently shows you what you've been carrying without judgment.

Quiz Flow

1

"Who are you?"

Two paths — "I live near my parents" (local caregiver) or "I live far from my parents" (NRI). This single question shapes the entire experience.

2

8–10 empathetic questions

Covering coordination, caregiving load, financial awareness, and sibling dynamics.

  • How do you coordinate with siblings? (WhatsApp? Calls? You don't?)
  • Who handles medical appointments? Bills? Groceries? Pharmacy runs?
  • Do you know if your parents' health insurance is active? When it renews?
  • How do you split costs? Is it fair? Does everyone agree it's fair?
  • When was the last time you had a real conversation about this with your siblings?
3

Gemini-powered "Family Care Reality" report

Personalized, warm, actionable. Four sections:

  • What you're carrying — estimated hours, estimated monthly cost
  • What's at risk — insurance gaps, financial blind spots, coordination gaps
  • What's going well — what your family already has figured out
  • One gentle nudge — "Families like yours often benefit from..."
Screenshot-worthy output

A warm, personal card — not a scary dashboard. Something you'd share in a family WhatsApp group or on LinkedIn as "I took this and it really opened my eyes."

Stretch Goal

Two User Paths

Local Caregiver

"Show your siblings what it really takes"

Create circle → log what you do → invite siblings to see the reality. The invisible labor becomes visible.

NRI / Distant Child

"Stay connected to your parents' care"

Create circle → contribute financially → see what your local sibling handles. Distance doesn't mean disconnection.

What it's NOT

  • Not Splitwise — no "Mom owes Dad" dynamics, no settle-up pressure
  • Not a spreadsheet — visual, mobile-first, minimal data entry
  • Not just money — time contribution counts equally

05 — Technical Scope

What to build

Stack

Next.js (App Router) Tailwind CSS Vercel Supabase Gemini 2.5 Flash

MVP Pages

1. Landing Page

Empathetic headline, "Take the 2-minute check" CTA. Mobile-first, fast, emotionally resonant.

2. Quiz Flow

8–10 questions, branching for local vs NRI path. One question per screen. Progress indicator.

3. Results Page

Gemini-generated personalized report + shareable card. The screenshot moment.

4. Family Circle

Create circle, invite siblings via shareable link. Anonymous — no accounts needed.

5. Expenses & Tasks

Log who paid/did what. 44px touch targets. Minimal data entry. Mobile-first forms.

6. Family Contacts

Shared contact book — doctor, driver, pharmacy, etc. Siblings add what they know.

7. Medical Records

Upload report photos + caregiver text updates. Tagged by date and type.

8. Circle Dashboard

Monthly view of contributions (time + money) + shareable "Family Care Update" card.

What NOT to Build

  • User accounts / login
  • Settings page
  • Admin dashboard
  • Dark mode
  • Notification system
  • Payment / billing
Supabase tracking

Each interaction is tracked in Supabase — quiz completions, circle creations, expense/task logs. This is how we count to 100 users. No separate analytics.

07 — Distribution

How 1 user becomes 100

📣

Entry: LinkedIn

Empathy-driven posts from team's personal accounts. Not stats-heavy, not guilt-inducing. Personal stories + "I took this check, here's what I learned."

🔄

Viral Loop

Quiz result is shareable → recipient takes quiz → shares their result. Family circle invite = 1 creator → 2–4 siblings. Natural compound growth.

Hook Tone

Authentic, empathy-first messaging. The research has the stats. The content should lead with the human story — not data dumps.
